3 Steps to Better Moka Pot Coffee

Quick Reference

Grind medium (not fine)
Pre-boil the water
Use an aeropress filter

Grind Medium (not fine)

The moka pot is sometimes called a stovetop espresso maker so it's natural to think you need a very fine grind. But this will lead to bitter, over-extracted coffee. Grinding medium will avoid this over-extraction for a flavorful but less bitter cup.

Pre-boil the water

Pre-boiling the water reduces the amount of time it takes to brew in the moka pot, which means the ground coffee spends less time being heated. This is another way to reduce bitterness in the brewed cup. It takes a little extra effort - you'll need to get some water boiling, then grind your beans and put in the funnel. Fill up the reservoir with the boiling water to just below the steam release, then put the funnel in. You might want a towel to hold the reservoir as you screw on the top, since the boiling water will quickly heat the reservoir.

Use an aeropress filter

Aeropress filters fit perfectly in the moka pot, right between the metal filter and the ground coffee. This creates slightly more pressure and prevents small particles from flowing up into the chamber. I'll even wash and re-use the filter a few times.
