French Press

The French Press is a coffee workhorse. In my house, it is one of two ways to make enough coffee for more than 1 person (Chemex is the other). It's simple, sustainable, pretty easy to clean, and makes coffee with a lot of body.

Quick Reference

  • 1:12 Ratio Coffee-to-Water
  • Course Grind
  • 200F/93C Water temperature

Example 2 Mug Brew

  1. Start boiling your water
  2. When the water is up to temp, grind 56g of coffee at a course setting (I grind lighter roasts a little finer and darker roasts as course as I can)
  3. Put the french press on a scale and put the ground coffee in it
  4. Tare the scale.
  5. Start a 3:30 timer, then start pouring water over the grounds until you reach a total of ~672g (1:12 ratio)
  6. When the timer goes off, slowly plunge, then pour into your mugs

Notes/Adjustments

  • My grinder has 3 settings for course - I grind lighter roasts at the lowest course setting and darker (even medium) roasts at the highest
  • I'll adjust the time to 3:00 for darker roasts
