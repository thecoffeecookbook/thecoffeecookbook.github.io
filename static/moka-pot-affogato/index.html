Moka Pot Affogato

Affogatos are one of the most delicious things in the entire universe. Hot espresso poured over a generous scoop of ice cream. You get the sensation of hot and cold, and the flavor fuses into sweet, creamy, caramel-y goodness. You won't find them at every coffee shop and they can be a little pricey - so why not make it at home?

I don't have an espresso machine in my house, so I turn to the moka pot - it's not true espresso but you can get a strong, concentrated coffee out of it and it's pretty close to the real thing. In a blind taste test, I don't think I could tell the difference.

You'll want your ice cream as cold as possible so it doesn't just melt into a sugar-y latte.

Tools/Ingredients

  • Moka pot
  • 2 bowls
  • 2 tiny spoons
  • Ice cream (Tillamook is good)
  • Coffee ground medium-course
  • Water to fill the moka pot reservoir half way

Directions

  1. Make your coffee in the moka pot, only fill it halfway with water (instead of up to the steam release). Check out the brew guide and the pro tips for the best moka pot coffee.
  2. Right as it's finishing, get your ice cream out of the freezer and scoop a nice big scoop into each of your bowls.
  3. When the coffee is done, pour it over the ice cream in the two bowls. I alternate between the two for consistency and to make sure they have roughly equal amounts of coffee.
  4. Dig in with those tiny spoons
